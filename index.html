Fun Facts about Bears

  1. A bear's diet consists of mostly meat and fish, but some species of bear also eat plants and insects.
  2. Black bears, found in North America, can reach speeds of up to 35 miles per hour.
  3. Baloo, Mowgli's friend from The Jungle Book, is a sloth bear.

Fun Facts about Camels

  • Camels are able to attain 7ft in height (at their hump) and can have a weight of up to 1500 pounds.
  • Camels have three eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to prevent sand from going in their eyes.
  • Two types of camels exist: Dromedary (one humped) and Bactrian (two humped).

Fun Facts about Chameleons

  1. Chameleons have skin crystals that allow them to change color in order to communicate with others and regulate body temperature.
  2. Almost 50 percent of all known chameleon species live in Madagascar.
  3. Chameleons have eyes that move independent of each other in order to locate fast-moving prey.

Fun Facts about Cicadas

  • Periodical cicadas in North America spend 13 to 17 years underground and then live aboveground for a few weeks.
  • The length of a cicada ranges from 0.75 inches to 2.25 inches and can have black, brown, or green bodies with red, white, or blue eyes.
  • There are about 3,000 species of cicadas.

Fun Facts about Pandas

  1. Pandas start off as pink without fur and then become black and white.
  2. Pandas only eat and sleep throughout their day.
  3. Adult Pandas can eat up to 12 - 38 kilograms of bamboo in a day.

Fun Facts about Pigeons

  • Pigeons have a highly sophisticated navigation abilities and can find their home from anywhere.
  • Pigeons are able to recognize all 26 letters of the English alphabet.
  • Pigeons were given cameras and used in WWII to take pictures of enemy bases.
